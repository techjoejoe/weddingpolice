Best Wedding Podcasts in 2026: Planning Tips, Wild Stories & What to Listen To

By Penny & Jim•March 2026Podcast Guide

Whether you just got engaged and need a podcast for wedding planning, or you're deep in the weeds of seating charts and need something unhinged to take the edge off — there's a wedding podcast out there for you. The problem? There are hundreds of them. Some are incredible. Some haven't posted since 2022. And some are… well, let's just say not every podcast host has actually planned a wedding.

We put together this guide based on what real people are actually searching for on Google, Bing, and other search engines — questions like "how to plan a wedding podcast," "podcast for wedding planning," and yes, even "who pooped at my wedding podcast." (We'll get to that one. Promise.)

What Makes a Great Wedding Podcast?

Before we dive into the list, here's what separates a truly great wedding podcast from the rest:

  • Real talk, not sales pitches. The best wedding podcasts give you honest opinions, not thinly-veiled vendor advertisements.
  • Consistent episodes. A lot of people search for "wedding podcast 2022" and end up finding shows that are long dead. You want a podcast that's actively producing episodes in 2026.
  • Entertaining hosts. You're listening to this while folding invitations or driving to cake tastings. The hosts better be fun.
  • Practical, actionable advice. Tips you can actually use — budget breakdowns, timeline checklists, vendor red flags.

🕵️ The Wedding Scammer Podcast

Another hugely searched topic: "the wedding scammer podcast." Wedding scams are unfortunately real — from fake photographers who disappear with deposits to venues that double-book and ghost you. Several podcast episodes and dedicated series cover these horror stories in true-crime style.

"Disney wedding podcast" is one of the most popular related searches, and we totally get why. Disney weddings are a massive niche — from Disneyland and Disney World venue options to Disney-themed décor on any budget. Dedicated Disney wedding podcasts cover everything:

How to Start Your Own Wedding Podcast

One of the top questions people search is "how to plan a wedding podcast" — and surprisingly, they're not always asking about wedding planning. Many people want to know how to actually start a wedding podcast of their own. Here's the quick version:

  • Pick your niche. "Wedding podcast" is broad. Are you covering planning tips? Real stories? A specific culture or religion? Budget weddings? Destination weddings? The more specific, the better.
  • Get basic equipment. You don't need a studio. A decent USB microphone ($50–$100), free editing software like Audacity, and a quiet room will get you started.
  • Record 3–5 episodes before launching. This gives new listeners something to binge and helps you work out the kinks.
  • Distribute everywhere. Upload to Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and YouTube at minimum. Use a host like Anchor (now Spotify for Podcasters) — it's free.
  • Be consistent. The #1 reason wedding podcasts die is inconsistency. Pick a schedule (weekly or biweekly) and stick to it.

We started The Wedding Police because we were sick of wedding advice that felt robotic and generic. If you have a unique perspective and genuine passion, there's absolutely room for another great wedding podcast.

Where to Listen to Wedding Podcasts

A common search is "the wedding where podcast" — which really means people are looking for where to find and listen to wedding podcasts. Here are all the major platforms:

"More or Less" Wedding Podcast & Comparison Shows

Some listeners are searching for shows like "more or less podcast wedding" — podcasts that compare wedding options and help you decide what's worth the splurge vs. what you can skip. This format is super helpful because it cuts through the noise and gives you direct comparisons:

  • Live band vs. DJ — which is actually better for your budget and vibe?
  • Buffet vs. plated dinner — what do guests actually prefer?
  • Big wedding vs. elopement — the real pros and cons nobody talks about
  • Real flowers vs. artificial — can guests even tell the difference?

On The Wedding Police, we do this constantly. Penny and Jim go head-to-head on the big wedding debates so you don't have to. It's like having two opinionated friends who've already done the research for you.

Are Older Wedding Podcasts Still Worth Listening To?

Lots of people search for "wedding podcast 2022" or older years — so here's the honest answer: some older episodes are timeless (speech advice, etiquette tips, relationship dynamics), but anything about pricing, trends, or vendor recommendations from 2022 or earlier is likely outdated. Wedding costs have shifted significantly since then, post-pandemic trends have settled, and vendor landscapes change fast.

Stick with podcasts that are actively producing episodes in 2026 for the most relevant advice. That said, binge-listening to a great podcast's back catalog is one of the best ways to absorb wedding wisdom — just take the numbers with a grain of salt.
