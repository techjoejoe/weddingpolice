What to Wear to a Summer Outdoor Wedding: The Complete Guide

By Penny & Jim • April 2026 Wedding Etiquette

Summer weddings are beautiful, but they present a unique wardrobe challenge. When the invitation says "outdoor ceremony," your first thought is probably about the heat, the humidity, and the grass. If you're wondering exactly what to wear to a summer outdoor wedding, you've come to the right place.

For a broad overview of general wedding attire, check out our comprehensive Wedding Guest Dress Code Guide. But for those specific summer challenges—like sinking heels and sweat stains—read on.

1. Choose the Right Fabrics

The secret to surviving a summer outdoor wedding is all in the fabric. Heavy synthetics like polyester will trap heat and leave you uncomfortable.

  • For Women: Look for lightweight silk, chiffon, linen blends, or high-quality cotton. Flowy midi or maxi dresses allow for air circulation.
  • For Men: Leave the heavy wool suit at home. Opt for a linen suit, seersucker, or a lightweight cotton blend. If the dress code allows, a light-colored suit (like tan or light blue) will reflect the sun rather than absorb it.

2. Master the "Grass-Friendly" Footwear

This is perhaps the most important rule for outdoor weddings: beware the grass!

  • Avoid Stilettos: Your heels will sink into the lawn, ruining your shoes and making it difficult to walk.
  • What to Wear Instead: Block heels, elegant wedges, or dressy flats are your best friends. If you absolutely must wear a thin heel, invest in plastic heel protectors.
  • For Men: Standard dress shoes are fine, but consider loafers (even sockless, if the dress code leans casual) for a breathable, summer-appropriate look.

3. Navigating Colors and Prints

Summer is the perfect time to embrace color. While our main dress code guide warns against wearing white (always!), summer outdoor weddings are the ideal setting for brights, pastels, and florals.

However, be cautious with light, solid colors (like light blue or pale pink) if you tend to sweat, as moisture shows up easily on these fabrics. Busy floral prints are excellent at hiding both wrinkles and sweat marks!

4. Prepare for the Elements

Outdoor weddings mean dealing with nature. Come prepared:

  • Sunglasses: A stylish pair of sunglasses is a must for a sunny ceremony.
  • The Evening Chill: Even hot summer days can turn chilly when the sun goes down. Bring a light pashmina, shawl, or a tailored linen blazer.
  • Sun Protection: Don't forget sunscreen! If the setting is very rustic, a chic wide-brimmed hat might be appropriate (just make sure it doesn't block the view of the person behind you during the ceremony).

Decoding the specific Summer Dress Code

Even if it's outdoors, the couple will likely still specify a dress code:

  • Summer Formal: Think lightweight elegance. A chiffon floor-length gown or a lighter-weight dark suit.
  • Summer Cocktail: A knee-length or midi dress in a bright color or floral print. A light-colored suit for men.
  • Garden Party / Beach Casual: Flowy maxi dresses, dressy rompers, linen trousers, and button-downs.
"The biggest mistake guests make at summer weddings is sacrificing comfort for style. If you're sweating profusely in a heavy wool suit, you won't have fun. Embrace linen!" — Jim, The Wedding Police
